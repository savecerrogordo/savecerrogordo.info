1. Cerro Gordo

Cerro Gordo is a 19th century mining (ghost) town.
At it's peak, it was the largest producer of silver in California.
Its abandoned mineshafts are full of old relics and important historical artifacts, with many shafts not having been touched for over a century.

1.1 Conglomerate Mesa

The Conglomerate Mesa is a mesa biome near Death Valley. It is situated around the area of Cerro Gordo and Keeler, and is a historic silver mining area from the late 19th/early 20th century, contributing to the development of cities like Los Angeles. It has a lot of unqiue flora and fauna and is adjacent to Death Valley.

2. Threat : K2 Gold Mine

K2 Gold Corporation is a mining corporation from Canada.
They own the Mojave Project gold mine, a giant open-pit cyanide gold mine, located directly next to (or potentially in) Cerro Gordo.
As a part of their expansions, they are now exploring directly into the Cerro Gordo and Conglomerate Mesa Area. Their mining method is using tons of cynaide dumped directly on public ground, with cyanide being a highly toxic material.

2.1 Why this matters

In the process of mining the proposed area, large quantities of historic artifacts and structures would be destroyed, hundreds of endangered plants and animals killed, and the ground forever contaminated. And all of this on public BLM owned land.
